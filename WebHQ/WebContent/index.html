Junior Java Developer
Mindteck
January 2015 – March 2015 | Madison, Wisconsin

Mindteck Academy -

I took part in an 8 week, client tailored, competitive training program focused on preparation for work on the Wisconsin CARES project managed by Deloitte.

The focus was on J2EE and applying common programming skills and practices to enterprise level problems and solutions - all while trying to use the architecture, design patterns, tech stack, and tools of the project which we were attempting to join. The training consisted of both lectures and hands on exercises, both alone and in teams. For the last 4 weeks I worked on a team of 4 to create a prototype web application and a supplementary demo and presentation. My team and I gave this presentation and demo to the leadership team at Deloitte as part of our final interview.

5 of 8 candidates received job offers.

Web Developer / Tech Support
The Cove Family Fun Center
June 2011 – August 2012 | Victorville, CA

The Whole Stack -

I was given the opportunity to conceive, design, and build a website from scratch for a local business. As time progressed and the site neared completion I spent more and more time providing general tech support such as hardware and software installation/upgrades.

This job was great because I had just completed my CIS degree and now I was able to work on the entire tech stack for this local business. I gained experience in html, css, javascript, php, sql, setting up hosting and domains, setting up networking, working with 3rd party hardware like receipt printers and card scanners, and many other things. It was a perfect opportunity to learn, improve, and see what my strengths and weaknesses were.

The business was closed after 1 year when funding was revoked.

Store Manager
Papa John's
November 2007 – November 2009 | Bullhead City, AZ

From Driver to Insider to Manager -

I first started work here as a part-time delivery driver. After a month or so I was moved into the store performing "insider" duties - mostly preparing food, cooking pizzas, and cleaning up. When the General Manager left to open his own store I was promoted to store manager where I remained for about 18 months. My main duties included interviewing, hiring, inventory, scheduling, handle customer complaints, local marketing, operational adjustments, shift leading, food preparation, and phone order taking.

I eventually left this job, moved to California, and returned to college to pursue a technology career.
